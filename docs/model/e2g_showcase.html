Signed sequence-based enhancer→gene modeling for GWAS effector-gene identification

Most GWAS loci are not resolved to their causal gene by expression-QTL colocalization. We ask whether a sequence-based, direction-aware enhancer→gene (E2G) model — built entirely on frozen DNA-language-model embeddings — can nominate effector genes and their regulatory sign where coloc is silent.

75.6% of resolved loci are not correctly resolved by cis-eQTL colocalization — either silent (44.7%) or nominating the wrong gene (30.9%). This is the space a sequence-based model is meant to address.

Caveat, stated plainly: "COLOC-SILENT" means no PP.H4≥0.8 expression-QTL hit in Open Targets 26.06 — not proof that no eQTL exists. It reflects the limits of current QTL tissue/cell-type panels. The silent fraction is the opportunity for a sequence model, bounded by those panels, not a claim of biological absence.

2The model: frozen embeddings → learned two-tower E2G with a sign head

Every model here consumes frozen Nucleotide-Transformer-v2-500M embeddings of 1 kb sequence windows — enhancer elements and gene-TSS windows — paired using DBNascent nascent-transcription signed E–G links (human 6.7M / mouse 9.4M pairs at FDR<0.01). No sequence model is fine-tuned; the learning is entirely in a lightweight two-tower head that also predicts the regulatory direction (activating / repressive).

A pooled-representation performance ceiling

Three architectures that pool each window to a single 1024-d vector — gradient-boosted trees (v4), an MLP two-tower (v5), and a 2-token cross-attention encoder (v5d) — all plateau at held-out LINK AUROC ≈ 0.61. The ceiling is a property of pooling away the 168 sequence positions, not of the prediction head. CATE keeps the per-token axis and is the one architecture with headroom; it is still training at the time of writing.

Reported honestly: v5 (the fully-trained model used for all utility analysis below) reaches held-out LINK AUPRC 0.583 / AUROC 0.611 and repressive-sign AUROC 0.605 on 4.0M held-out pairs (human chr16/2/22/9 + mouse chr2/9/X). v5d reaches 0.621 AUROC on validation. CATE's held-out number is pending epoch completion.

3Utility I — recovering regulatory links, and their direction

On the held-out test split (7,666 genes with ≥2 candidate enhancers), the model ranks the correct enhancer first for a gene at precision@1 = 0.729, versus 0.719 for a nearest-enhancer baseline. The margin over distance is modest — but the model additionally predicts the regulatory sign:

Sign prediction is the distinctive capability: colocalization and distance tell you which gene, never whether the regulatory effect is activating or repressive. On 1.7M confident held-out links the model separates repressive from activating/neutral at AUROC 0.605.

4Utility II — effector nomination at GWAS loci, including coloc-silent ones

For each benchmark locus we anchor on the nearest DBNascent enhancer element to the sentinel variant, score it against every candidate gene's TSS with the signed E2G model, and take the top-ranked gene as the nomination — stratified by colocalization status.

The honest result: nearest-gene is a strong baseline here (47.7% of effectors) and out-performs naive E2G nomination in aggregate (E2G 22.1%). This is expected — the model was trained to rank enhancers for a gene, not to pick genes for a variant-tagged locus, so locus nomination is an out-of-distribution use. The value is complementary: E2G correctly nominates 39 effectors the distance baseline gets wrong, lifting the union recovery to 51.1% (62.9% at coloc-silent loci), and it does so with an interpretable regulatory link plus a direction call.

5What this shows, and what it doesn't

  • Shown: cis-eQTL coloc leaves ~76% of curated GWAS effector loci unresolved; a frozen-embedding signed E2G model recovers held-out regulatory links (p@1 0.729) and their direction (repressive AUROC 0.605); and at coloc-silent loci it recovers 18 known effectors that nearest-gene misses, including drug-target genes.
  • Not shown: that E2G beats distance for blanket locus-level gene nomination — it does not, in this out-of-distribution setup. The contribution is complementary recovery plus direction, not replacement.
  • The bridge / next step: proper variant→enhancer anchoring (fine-mapped credible-set elements rather than nearest DBNascent element) and completing the per-token CATE model, whose per-position representation is the one architecture not capped by the pooling ceiling.

Methods & provenance

Embeddings: Nucleotide-Transformer-v2-500M (frozen), 1 kb windows, 168 tokens. Training pairs: DBNascent signed E–G links (FDR<0.01), human+mouse combined, chromosome-held-out splits. Model v5: two-tower MLP over PCA-613 embeddings, masked multitask heads (link / 3-way sign / element / splice); 0.78M parameters. Effector benchmark: Open Targets Genetics L2G gold standard (Mountjoy et al. 2021, Nat Genet, doi:10.1038/s41588-021-00945-5); colocalization from Open Targets Platform 26.06 (COLOC_PIP_ECAVIAR, h4≥0.8); candidate genes ±500 kb from Ensembl GRCh38. All held-out metrics computed on chromosomes withheld from training.
